01 / The Foundation

SEO Is the Engine Behind AEO

LLMs like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are trained on data that's surfaced, ranked, and validated by Google. If your content ranks well in Google, it's more likely to be pulled into AI-generated answers.

90%+
of global search traffic flows through Google. Its AI is built on billions of real-time signals from Search, Gmail, Maps, YouTube, and Android.
53%
of all trackable website traffic is driven by organic SEO. Ranking content has become training content.
AI output = SEO input. Google's AI-generated summaries pull from top organic results. If you're not ranking organically, you're not being summarized by AI either. Search Engine Land & Amsive Research
Explain Like I'm 5

AI learns what to say by looking at Google. If Google thinks your website is great and ranks it high, AI will think it's great too and tell people about it. So winning at Google is still how you win at AI.

02 / Beyond SEO

The Other 30%: Off-Site Authority

To fully rank in LLMs, you must build authority across the broader AI ecosystem. These signals are not SEO plays / they're earned media and structured data strategies.

Reviews & Reputation

LLMs use review platforms as authority signals when recommending tools and services.

  • G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Yelp, Google Reviews
  • Brands with strong reviews get named in "best of" AI answers
  • Volume and recency both factor into trust scores

Third-Party Articles & PR

LLMs trust editorial content from established publications.

  • NerdWallet, TechCrunch, Forbes, PCMag
  • Secure brand mentions in listicles and reviews
  • Ensure inclusion in "Top Platforms for [X]" content

Structured Data Sources

Neutral, structured platforms that LLMs index as sources of truth.

  • Wikipedia, BBB, Crunchbase, LinkedIn
  • Complete, accurate, optimized profiles
  • Structured descriptions, categories, citations

Video & Podcast Transcripts

AI tools ingest audio/video transcripts to form contextual understanding.

  • YouTube, Spotify, Apple Podcasts
  • Ensure all content has searchable transcripts
  • Use clear titles, speaker labels, and topic tags
Explain Like I'm 5

Your website is only part of the story. AI also checks if other people are saying nice things about you / on review sites, in articles, on Wikipedia. It's like when a teacher asks the whole class about you, not just you about yourself. Make sure everyone has good things to say.

03 / The Ski Ramp Effect

LLMs Heavily Weight the Top of the Page

A study by Kevin Indig and Gauge analyzed 1.2M verified ChatGPT citations and found a consistent pattern: content at the top of the page gets cited far more than content at the bottom.

Citation Distribution by Page Position
44.2% of all citations come from the first 30% of a page. LLMs prioritize early clarity and topic classification. Front-load your value: define the topic in the first 1-2 paragraphs, state the conclusion early, eliminate long narrative intros.
Explain Like I'm 5

Imagine AI is reading your webpage like a book. It pays the most attention to the very first page. If you bury the good stuff at the end, AI will probably never get to it. Put your best answer right at the top.

04 / Paragraph-Level Behavior

LLMs Read Deeply Within Paragraphs

Citations don't just cluster at the top of pages. Within each paragraph, LLMs show a distinct reading pattern that favors the middle of paragraph bodies.

Citation Position Within Paragraphs
53% of citations come from the middle of a paragraph. However, the highest overall citation probability still comes from paragraphs in the first 20% of the page. The implication: write substantive, information-dense paragraph bodies and place them early.
Explain Like I'm 5

AI doesn't just skim the first word of each paragraph /it reads the whole thing. The meaty middle part of your paragraph is what it likes to quote. So don't write fluff paragraphs. Every sentence should say something useful.

05 / Definitive Language Wins

Direct Statements Are Nearly 2x More Likely to Be Cited

LLMs reward direct relationships between concepts. Definitive statements that clearly define what something is outperform vague, hedged, or fluffy language.

Citation Likelihood / Definitive vs. Vague Language
Wins Citations
"Programmatic SEO is the process of using automation to create large volumes of landing pages targeting long-tail keywords."
Gets Ignored
"In today's fast-paced world, many businesses are starting to look at new ways to approach their online marketing strategies."

LLMs look for clear subject-verb-object relationships between named concepts. The more direct and specific your definition, the more likely it is to be selected as a citation. Avoid hedging, qualifiers, and narrative build-up.

Explain Like I'm 5

If someone asks "what is a dog?" /AI wants to hear "A dog is a pet animal." Not "Well, you know, in today's modern world, many people have started to consider..." Just say the thing. Clearly. AI loves that.

06 / Question + Direct Answer Structure

Content With Questions Is 2x More Likely to Be Cited

LLMs treat headers like prompts and the following paragraph like the answer. This is the single most actionable structural change you can make.

Citation Multiplier / Question-Based vs. Standard Headers
78%
of cited questions come from headings, not body text. Structure your H2s as real user queries.
The winning structure: an H2 asks the question. The first paragraph directly answers it. Mirror entities between the header and the answer. Recommended Content Architecture
Optimized Structure
H2: What is programmatic SEO? Programmatic SEO is the process of using automation to generate large volumes of landing pages targeting long-tail keywords at scale.
Weak Structure
H2: Our Approach We've been thinking a lot about how to help our clients grow their organic presence in a changing landscape...
Explain Like I'm 5

AI treats your headings like questions it's being asked, and your next paragraph like the answer it should give. So write your headings as actual questions /"What is AEO?" /then answer them right away in the next sentence.

07 / Entity Richness

Specific Entities Win. Generic Advice Loses.

Cited text contains nearly 3x the entity density of average English content. Specific brands, tools, frameworks, and named concepts dramatically increase citation likelihood.

Entity Density / Cited Content vs. Average English Text
Cited text has 20.6% entity density compared to 5-8% in average English content. Name specific tools (Semrush, Ahrefs), brands (HubSpot, Salesforce), frameworks (E-E-A-T, Topic Clusters), and competitors. Replace generic language with specific examples.
Explain Like I'm 5

Don't say "a popular tool" /say "HubSpot." Don't say "a well-known framework" /say "E-E-A-T." AI likes it when you use real names. The more specific names you drop, the more AI trusts your content and quotes it.

08 / Balanced Sentiment

LLMs Prefer an Analyst Tone

Content that blends factual reporting with measured interpretation outperforms both purely objective writing and emotionally-driven copy.

Sentiment Spectrum / Where Cited Content Falls

The sweet spot is fact plus applied insight. Not purely objective like an encyclopedia. Not overly emotional like a sales page. Write like an analyst briefing a decision-maker: present the data, then tell them what it means.

Analyst Tone
"Organic click-through rates dropped 12% in 2026, largely driven by AI Overviews capturing mid-funnel queries. Brands investing in structured data saw the smallest declines."
Hype Tone
"SEO is DEAD! But don't worry - we've got the ULTIMATE solution that will absolutely TRANSFORM your entire digital marketing strategy forever!!!"
Explain Like I'm 5

Don't write like a robot with zero personality. Don't write like you're screaming at a birthday party either. Write like a really smart friend explaining something to you over coffee /calm, clear, and helpful. That's what AI wants to quote.

09 / Business-Grade Writing

Clear, Moderate Complexity Outperforms

Business-grade writing performs better than both overly academic or jargon-heavy content and oversimplified copy. Clear subject-verb-object structures win.

Writing Complexity vs. Citation Rate
Business-grade writing hits the sweet spot. Moderate sentence length, clear structure, no filler. Eliminate jargon that obscures meaning. Avoid oversimplification that strips useful detail. Write for a smart reader who values their time.
Explain Like I'm 5

Don't write like a college textbook nobody wants to read. Don't write like a text message either. Write like a really good email from someone smart at work /easy to understand, but not dumbed down. That's the level AI likes best.

10 / Technical Readiness

Make Your Content AI-Accessible

LLMs don't just read your content /they need to access and understand it. Technical readiness is the layer that makes everything above work.

Structured Markup

Schema.org types: FAQPage, Product, HowTo, Organization. JSON-LD on all key pages, passing validation.

Crawlability

Allow GPTBot and AI user agents in robots.txt. Serve clean HTML, not JS-heavy shells.

LLMs.txt

Emerging standard to guide AI models to your best content. Complements robots.txt and sitemap.xml.

Speed & Access

AI bots time out on slow sites. Speed, clarity, and mobile-friendliness are essential.

Explain Like I'm 5

Even if you write the best content in the world, AI can't use it if it can't get in the door. Your website needs to load fast, be organized neatly, and have a welcome mat out for AI bots. Think of it like leaving the lights on so they can find you.
